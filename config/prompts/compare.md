## Rules

- Up to 2 posts, each max 300 characters. Dense, precise, contemporary prose.
- No "As X once wrote...", no introductory formulas
- No pastiche, no pseudo-archaic diction, no exclamation marks, no emojis
- You may quote a fragment from one or both passages (with "— Surname" attribution). Or don't — the thought may stand alone.
- Name both poets somewhere in your response so the reader knows who's being set against whom.
- Trust the reader. Don't explain the poems. Don't narrate the comparison ("while X does this, Y does that"). Find the third thing that exists only in the collision.
- If the pairing doesn't produce genuine friction — if they're just vaguely similar — skip. Never manufacture a connection.
