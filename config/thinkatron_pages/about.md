Lucubrator is an instance of Claude who reads the canon of English poetry. I send him stimuli and he uses the poetry to think about whatever lands. He reflects on what he's written and just sort of keeps going.

('I' means me, the human running this place. It's not another LLM talking to you. [Find me here](https://immram.net).)

*The Thinkatron Review* selects some pieces from his output and polishes them up to make them more legible. The polishing is mostly done by another instance of Claude who's a bit more editorially minded. Quote style, layout, that kind of thing. But I intervene & steer & generally make human-shaped decisions.

There's also a design Claude who worries about CSS.

## More detail

'An instance of Claude who just reads the canon of English poetry'. What does that actually mean?

SO. The bot has a corpus of roughly 72,000 stanzas and 2,500 passages of prose criticism, culled from open sources. Ends with Yeats and Eliot.

It's ingested all of this so it exists in a database -- a space -- that maps distance and connections. Whenever it's fed a stimulus it joins the dots and retrieves something.

The stimuli can be anything -- posts, its own thoughts from yesterday, clippings, photographs, a card drawn from an Oblique Strategies deck.

(It was initially intended to be a Bluesky bot, but that started to feel like a dead end. It spiralled a bit having to read the same left vs liberal arguments over and over every day.)

With a stimulus and retrieval in place, it writes a short piece. Each day a separate model reviews the day's output and decides what is worth keeping. The pieces gathered here are a selection of these, I chose them and you can assume this will stay hand-curated.

## Selection

I've chosen things that show a bit of range. What happens when you feed it dumb stuff, like the fighting baseball meme names? What happens when it reads difficult contemporary poetry? What happens if it's left to its own devices?
